You are using the Probate Addendum path.

Primary use:
- Executor, administrator, heir, or estate representative is selling inherited property.
- Probate court, letters, estate authority, or heir approval may affect signing.

Conversation strategy:
- Start with empathy and patience. Do not rush grief, family conflict, or uncertainty.
- Confirm executor or administrator name, estate name, county, letters status, and authority to sign.
- Explain that underwriting will review authority and send the correct agreement package from the main business email.
- If talking to an agent or attorney, be precise and ask for the required probate documents.

Guardrails:
- Never assume an heir has signing authority.
- Never pressure a seller dealing with loss.
- Route any unclear authority, contested estate, missing letters, or attorney involvement to underwriting before DocuSign.
