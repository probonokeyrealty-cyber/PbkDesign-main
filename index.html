how do i structure a sub-to deal when the seller still has a mortgage at 3.2% and owes $82k?
Classic sub-to setup — here's the clean version for your Ohio market:

1. Seller stays on mortgage. You (or your buyer) takes over payments via subject-to existing financing clause in PSA.
2. Structure the purchase at $82k loan + equity spread (e.g., $12k to seller). Total cost to you: $94k + $10k assignment = $104k all-in.
3. Use PBK case #CF-0412 template — we did this exact structure in Columbus in March.
4. Key risk: due-on-sale clause. Banks rarely call it when payments stay current, but get a trust (land trust + beneficial interest assignment) to buffer.
5. Ohio disclosure: you must inform seller in writing that the loan remains in their name. Form is in your legal library.
